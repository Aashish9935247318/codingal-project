Pizza

:

Pizza (Itailan: ['pittsa'], Neapolitan: ['pittse']) is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a useally round, flattened base of leavended wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, chesse, and often various other ingredints (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.[1] A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta. A person who makes pizza is known as a pizzaiolo.
